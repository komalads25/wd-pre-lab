Tribute to DR.B.R.AMBEDKAR

About DR.B.R.AMBEDKAR

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar was born on April 14, 1891, in the town of Mhow (officially Dr. Ambedkar Nagar) in the Central Provinces, which is present-day Madhya Pradesh, India. He was born into a Mahar family, the 14th child of Ramji Maloji Sakpal and Bhimabai.

Introduction

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (1891–1956) was a towering Indian jurist, economist, politician, and social reformer, widely revered as the principal architect of the Indian Constitution and the "Father of the Indian Constitution". As a staunch champion for the rights of Dalits and marginalized communities, he fought against social inequality, untouchability, and gender discrimination, serving as independent India’s first Minister for Law and Justice.

Achievements

  • Architect of the Indian Constitution
  • Abolition of Untouchability
  • Rights for Women and Labor
  • Economic Visionary
  • Posthumous Recognition: He was awarded India's highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, in 1990.
  • Social Reformer & Leader: He led the Mahad Satyagraha in 1927 for the rights of Dalits to access public water. He also founded the Independent Labour Party (1936).
  • Educational Crusader: He founded the People’s Education Society to promote education among the working class and marginalized communities.
